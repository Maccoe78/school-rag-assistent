Attendance Warning Process

Purpose
This document explains how Nova Tech College handles attendance warnings when a student’s attendance becomes a concern.

When a Warning May Be Issued
A warning may be issued when a student:
- has repeated unauthorized absence
- frequently arrives late
- misses multiple mandatory sessions
- shows a pattern of poor attendance affecting progress

Types of Warnings
The school may use:
- informal warning by a teacher or mentor
- formal attendance notice
- required meeting with support staff
- written follow-up plan

Purpose of a Warning
The goal of an attendance warning is to:
- inform the student that attendance is problematic
- identify causes
- discuss improvement steps
- prevent further study delay

Student Response
Students are expected to:
- attend requested meetings
- explain relevant circumstances honestly
- follow reasonable improvement agreements
- seek support where needed

Escalation
If attendance does not improve after warnings, the school may take further measures according to internal study progress procedures.

Contact
Questions about attendance warnings should be directed to the academic mentor.
