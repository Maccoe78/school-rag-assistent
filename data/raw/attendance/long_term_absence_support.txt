Long-Term Absence Support

Purpose
This document explains how Nova Tech College supports students who are absent for a longer period due to illness, personal issues, or other serious circumstances.

Definition
Long-term absence generally refers to absence that continues for multiple days or repeatedly affects participation and study progress.

Support Principles
The school aims to support students in a realistic and structured way while maintaining educational standards.

Possible Support Measures
Depending on the situation, support may include:
- contact with the academic mentor
- referral to student support services
- adjusted planning where possible
- temporary study arrangements
- guidance on catching up with coursework

Student Responsibility
Students remain responsible for:
- maintaining communication with the school
- providing relevant updates when possible
- discussing realistic study planning
- following agreed support arrangements

Confidentiality
Personal information is handled carefully and shared only with staff members who need the information to provide support.

Limitations
Not all activities can be replaced or postponed. Practical assessments, internships, and formal exams may have separate rules.

Contact
Students experiencing long-term absence should contact their academic mentor as soon as possible.
