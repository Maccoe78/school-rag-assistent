Unauthorized Absence Policy

Purpose
This document explains what counts as unauthorized absence and what the consequences may be at Nova Tech College.

Definition
Unauthorized absence means a student did not attend a scheduled required activity and did not follow the correct reporting procedure or did not have an accepted reason.

Examples of Unauthorized Absence
Unauthorized absence may include:
- missing class without reporting
- leaving early without permission
- arriving so late that meaningful participation is no longer possible
- failing to attend a mandatory meeting or workshop without notice
- using an invalid or misleading reason for absence

Consequences
Unauthorized absence may lead to:
- registration in the attendance system
- a warning from the school
- a meeting with the academic mentor
- mandatory catch-up arrangements
- consequences for practical components or progress reviews

Repeated Unauthorized Absence
Repeated unauthorized absence may trigger:
- formal monitoring
- referral to support staff
- written warning
- further institutional action under attendance and progress rules

Student Responsibility
Students are expected to understand when attendance is mandatory and how absence must be reported.

Exceptional Cases
If a student believes an absence was incorrectly marked as unauthorized, the student should contact the school promptly and provide clarification.

Contact
Questions about unauthorized absence should be directed to the academic mentor or student administration.
