Oral Exam Guidelines
Purpose
This document explains the rules and expectations for oral exams, oral defenses, and presentation-based assessments at Nova Tech College.

Nature of Oral Assessments
An oral assessment may be used to evaluate:

subject knowledge
communication skills
professional reasoning
reflection on process and choices
defense of project work or portfolio content
Scheduling
Oral exams are scheduled individually or in small groups.
Students must check the time, location, and any preparation requirements in advance.
Missing an oral exam without a valid reason may count as a missed attempt.
Identification
Students may be asked to identify themselves before the oral assessment begins.

Conduct During the Exam
Students are expected to:

answer honestly and independently
remain respectful and professional
focus on the questions asked
provide examples or explanations where requested
Use of Materials
Supporting materials may only be used if this is permitted in the assessment instructions.
Unauthorized notes, online tools, or external help are not allowed.
Assessment Criteria
Oral exams may be judged on:

clarity of explanation
understanding of key concepts
ability to justify choices
correctness of answers
communication and structure
professional attitude
Recording and Notes
The assessor may take notes during the oral exam for grading purposes. Audio or video recording is not allowed unless explicitly announced and approved.

Contact
Questions about oral assessments should be directed to the teacher or Exam Office.
