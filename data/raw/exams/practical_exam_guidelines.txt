Practical Exam Guidelines
Purpose
This document describes the rules for practical exams and performance-based assessments at Nova Tech College.

Definition
A practical exam assesses the student’s ability to apply knowledge and skills in a realistic task, project, lab, or technical working environment.

Preparation
Students must:

review the assignment instructions in advance
bring required materials or software if stated
ensure they understand the practical assessment criteria
arrive on time and be ready to begin at the scheduled moment
During the Practical Exam
Students must follow all task instructions and safety requirements.
Students may be observed while performing the task.
Communication with other students is only allowed if collaboration is explicitly part of the assignment.
Unauthorized external help is not allowed.
Work must be attributable to the student or team as instructed.
Technical Requirements
Where applicable, students are responsible for:

using approved devices and software
saving work correctly
following file naming or submission instructions
reporting technical problems immediately
Assessment Criteria
Practical exams may be assessed on:

technical correctness
process quality
independence
professional attitude
time management
documentation
final output quality
Safety and Conduct
Students must comply with safety instructions in workshops, labs, studios, or technical classrooms. Unsafe behavior may lead to immediate exclusion.

Absence or Interruption
If a practical exam cannot be completed due to illness, technical failure, or another serious issue, the student must report the situation immediately. A resit is not automatic and depends on the circumstances.

Contact
Questions about practical exams should be directed to the course teacher or Exam Office.
