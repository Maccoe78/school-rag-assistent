General Exam Regulations
Purpose
This document describes the general rules for exams, tests, assessments, and other formal evaluation moments at Nova Tech College.

Scope
These regulations apply to all enrolled students in programs offered by Nova Tech College, including theoretical exams, practical exams, projects, presentations, and oral assessments.

General Rules
Students are responsible for checking the exam schedule through the official student portal.
Students must arrive on time and bring a valid student ID to every formal assessment.
Students must follow instructions given by invigilators, teachers, and exam coordinators.
Only approved materials and tools may be used during an exam.
Communication with other students during an exam is not allowed unless explicitly permitted.
Mobile phones, smartwatches, and other communication devices must be switched off and stored away unless their use is explicitly approved.
Students are expected to behave professionally and respectfully during all assessment moments.
Participation Conditions
A student may only participate in an exam if they are properly enrolled in the course or module.
A student may be denied access to an assessment if they cannot identify themselves.
A student may be denied participation if they arrive too late, depending on the type of exam and the applicable late-arrival rules.
A student must meet any published submission requirements before participating in practical or project-based assessments.
Assessment Types
Nova Tech College uses different forms of assessment, including:

written exams
digital exams
practical assignments
presentations
project evaluations
oral exams
portfolio reviews
Responsibilities of the Student
Students must:

prepare for assessments using the official course materials
review the applicable instructions before an exam
submit required work before deadlines
report issues or exceptional circumstances as soon as possible
comply with all academic integrity rules
Responsibilities of the School
Nova Tech College will:

provide clear information about assessment formats and dates
communicate rules and expectations in advance
organize assessments in a fair and structured manner
register grades in the student information system
provide access to review procedures where applicable
Exceptional Circumstances
If a student cannot attend or complete an assessment due to illness, technical failure, or another serious circumstance, the student must report this according to the applicable procedures. Supporting evidence may be required.

Violations
Violations of exam rules may lead to:

invalidation of the exam attempt
exclusion from the assessment
reporting to the exam committee
disciplinary action under the fraud and plagiarism policy
Contact
Questions about general exam regulations can be directed to the Exam Office or the student’s academic mentor.
