Grading and Assessment Rules
Purpose
This document explains how grades and assessment outcomes are determined and registered at Nova Tech College.

General Grading System
Assessments may be graded numerically, descriptively, or on a pass/fail basis.
The grading method is stated in the course information or assessment instructions.
A grade is only valid once it has been officially registered in the student information system.
Sufficient and Insufficient Results
A result is considered sufficient when it meets the minimum standard set for the assessment.
For numerical grades, the passing threshold is normally 5.5 or higher, unless a course specifies another standard.
For pass/fail assessments, all required criteria must be met.
Assessment Criteria
Teachers assess work based on published criteria, which may include:

correctness
completeness
technical quality
professional conduct
communication
documentation
problem-solving ability
Weighting
Some courses combine multiple assessments into one final result.
Weighting percentages must be communicated in advance.
If weighting applies, the final result is calculated according to the published structure.
Missing Components
A final grade may not be registered as sufficient if required parts are missing, even when other parts are completed at a good level.

Publication of Results
Results are normally published through the student portal.
Students are expected to review their results within a reasonable time after publication.
Correction of Errors
If a grading or registration error is discovered:

the teacher or department may correct the error
the student will be informed if the registered result changes
documented evidence must support the correction
Questions and Disputes
If a student disagrees with a grade, the student should first discuss the result with the teacher. If needed, the student may use the formal review or objection procedure.

Contact
Questions about grading should be directed to the relevant teacher or the Exam Office.
