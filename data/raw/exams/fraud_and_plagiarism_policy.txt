Fraud and Plagiarism Policy
Purpose
This document sets out the rules concerning fraud, plagiarism, unauthorized assistance, and academic misconduct at Nova Tech College.

Core Principle
All assessment work submitted by a student must be honest, original where required, and completed according to the applicable rules.

Examples of Fraud
Fraud includes, but is not limited to:

using unauthorized notes or tools during an exam
copying answers from another student
sharing answers during a test
impersonating another student
submitting work completed by someone else
manipulating digital systems to alter results or submissions
Examples of Plagiarism
Plagiarism includes:

copying text without proper acknowledgment
reusing another person’s code, report, or design as if it were your own
submitting AI-generated or externally produced work as fully original personal work when not permitted
paraphrasing a source too closely without attribution
AI Use
The use of AI tools is only allowed when:

the teacher or course guide explicitly permits it
the student follows all disclosure requirements
the submitted work still reflects the student’s own understanding and contribution
If AI use is not allowed or is not properly disclosed, it may be treated as fraud or plagiarism.

Investigation Procedure
If fraud or plagiarism is suspected:

the incident is documented by the teacher or invigilator
the student may be asked for an explanation
the case may be forwarded to the exam committee
the school reviews evidence before making a formal decision
Possible Measures
Depending on severity, the school may impose:

invalidation of the assessment
a grade of insufficient
exclusion from a resit
formal warning
referral to the exam committee
further disciplinary action
Repeat Offenses
Repeated academic misconduct is treated more seriously and may lead to stronger sanctions.

Student Responsibility
Students are responsible for understanding:

what counts as original work
when collaboration is allowed
how sources must be cited
whether AI tools are permitted in a specific assignment
Contact
Questions about academic integrity should be directed to the teacher, academic mentor, or Exam Office.
