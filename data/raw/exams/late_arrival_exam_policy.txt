Late Arrival Exam Policy
Purpose
This document explains the rules for students who arrive late to an exam or assessment at Nova Tech College.

General Rule
Students must arrive on time for all assessments. Being late may affect whether participation is still allowed.

Written and Digital Exams
A student who arrives within 15 minutes after the official start time may be allowed to enter, if this does not disturb the exam process.
After 15 minutes, access may be refused.
No extra time is granted for late arrival unless the school determines that the delay was caused by an organizational issue.
Oral Exams and Presentations
For oral exams, presentations, and scheduled individual assessments, late arrival may result in cancellation of the appointment.
The assessor may decide whether participation is still possible, depending on the schedule.
Practical Assessments
For practical exams, late arrival may lead to exclusion if the assessment setup, safety process, or planning cannot be adjusted.
Students must follow any special instructions provided for labs, workshops, or technical exam environments.
Valid Exceptional Circumstances
In exceptional cases, such as:

sudden public transport disruption
emergency medical situation
other serious and provable circumstances
the school may decide to treat the late arrival as an excused case. Supporting evidence may be requested.

Student Responsibility
Students are responsible for:

checking the correct time and location in advance
allowing enough travel time
reporting serious delays as soon as possible
Consequences
If participation is refused due to late arrival:

the assessment may be recorded as missed
the student may need to request a resit
the final status depends on whether the reason is accepted
Contact
For questions about late arrival, students should contact the Exam Office.
