Internship Problem Reporting

Purpose
This document explains what students should do if problems arise during internship.

Examples of Problems
Problems may include:
- lack of guidance
- unsafe working conditions
- conflict with colleagues or supervisors
- tasks that do not match the internship goals
- discrimination, intimidation, or harassment
- repeated scheduling or communication issues

General Rule
Students should report internship problems as early as possible. Waiting too long may make the issue harder to solve.

First Step
Where appropriate and safe, the student should first discuss the issue with the workplace supervisor.

When to Contact the School
The student should contact the school immediately if:
- the issue is serious
- the student feels unsafe
- the issue cannot be resolved at the workplace
- there is a risk to internship progress or wellbeing

School Response
The school may:
- discuss the issue with the student
- contact the internship provider
- review whether the placement remains suitable
- support a change of placement if necessary

Contact
Urgent internship concerns should be directed to the internship coordinator or school supervisor.
