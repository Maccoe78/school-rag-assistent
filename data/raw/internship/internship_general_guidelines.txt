Internship General Guidelines

Purpose
This document describes the general rules and expectations for internships at Nova Tech College.

Definition
An internship, also referred to as BPV, is a supervised work-based learning period in which the student develops professional skills in a real or realistic work environment.

General Expectations
Students are expected to:
- behave professionally
- follow workplace rules
- communicate respectfully with supervisors and colleagues
- complete agreed learning tasks
- represent Nova Tech College in a responsible way

Learning Objectives
An internship is intended to help the student:
- apply knowledge from the study program
- develop practical and professional skills
- learn workplace communication
- reflect on strengths and improvement points
- gain experience relevant to the field of study

Approval Requirement
An internship must be approved by the school before it officially counts toward the program. Students may not start an unapproved internship and assume it will automatically be accepted.

Student Responsibility
The student is responsible for:
- finding or accepting a suitable internship placement
- maintaining communication with the school
- completing required internship assignments
- reporting issues in time
- attending as agreed with the internship provider

School Responsibility
The school is responsible for:
- reviewing internship suitability
- assigning school-based supervision
- monitoring progress where applicable
- supporting the student in case of serious issues

Contact
Questions about general internship rules should be directed to the internship coordinator.
