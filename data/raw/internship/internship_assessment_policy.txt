Internship Assessment Policy

Purpose
This document explains how internships are assessed at Nova Tech College.

General Principle
An internship is assessed based on both the learning process and the demonstrated professional performance of the student.

Possible Assessment Components
Internship assessment may include:
- attendance and completed hours
- workplace performance
- professional attitude
- internship assignments
- reflection reports
- feedback from the internship supervisor
- evaluation by the school supervisor

Assessment Criteria
Students may be assessed on:
- reliability
- communication
- technical or professional skills
- independence
- collaboration
- learning attitude
- ability to reflect on development

Insufficient Performance
An internship may be assessed as insufficient if:
- the student fails to meet core expectations
- required documents are missing
- completed hours are insufficient
- serious professional conduct issues occur
- required assignments are not submitted

Feedback
Students are expected to discuss progress regularly with both the internship provider and the school supervisor where applicable.

Contact
Questions about internship assessment should be directed to the internship coordinator or school supervisor.
